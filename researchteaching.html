Research and Teaching

As an astrophysicist-in-training, I focus on gravitational waves and teach undergraduate physics courses.

Research

I was always fond of astronomy and the night sky since they remind us that we're only observers during our time on earth. I was fortunate enough to have professors and mentors who would introduce me to a new and exciting field: Gravitational-Wave (GW) Astronomy. As opposed to traditional Tstronomy which uses electromagnetic (EM) radiation, GW Astronomy relies on gravitational radiation, a product of Einstein's genius and now a regular observable thanks to the global efforts of GW astronomers.

As a graduate student researcher, I am currently involved in a research project with Dr. Reinabelle Reyes concerning uncertainty modeling of astrophysical environments in the Laser Interferometer Space Antenna (LISA) band. We used Fisher matrix and overlap analysis
